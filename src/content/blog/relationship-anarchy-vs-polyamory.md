Polyamory and relationship anarchy are often mentioned in the same breath – and occasionally confused. Both belong to ethical non-monogamy; both break with the idea that an exclusive two-person relationship is the only valid form. Yet they describe different things. This article explains the differences clearly and helps you work out what fits you.

## The short answer

**Polyamory** describes *how many* relationships you have: multiple simultaneous romantic relationships, consensually and with everyone's knowledge.

**Relationship anarchy** describes *how* you think about relationships fundamentally: without fixed categories, without hierarchies, and without preset rules that make one type of relationship automatically more important than another.

Put differently: polyamory is a relationship structure; relationship anarchy is a relationship philosophy. You can be poly without being a relationship anarchist – and vice versa.

## Polyamory in detail

Polyamory is about multiple parallel romantic relationships. Many polyamorous people work with structures: there might be a nesting partner they live with and further relationships alongside. Some polyamorous constellations are explicitly **hierarchical** – with primary and secondary partners where certain relationships take precedence. Others are **egalitarian** and treat all relationships as equally valued. The key point: polyamory says nothing about whether you reject or use hierarchies. For more on the core vocabulary, see our [polyamory glossary](/blog/polyamory-glossary).

## Relationship anarchy in detail

Relationship anarchy (RA) goes a step further and questions the categories themselves. The core idea: no relationship should automatically rank above another just because it is romantic or sexual. A deep friendship can be just as central as a love relationship. Instead of following social templates, every connection is negotiated individually – based on the needs of those involved, not a fixed script.

Important: relationship anarchy does not mean "no commitment" or "no rules". It means the absence of predetermined hierarchy – not the absence of responsibility. Relationship anarchists absolutely do make agreements; they simply take them on consciously rather than assuming them as given.

## Key differences at a glance

**Focus:** Polyamory is about the number of romantic relationships. Relationship anarchy is about how all relationships are conceptualised and organised.

**Hierarchy:** Polyamory can be hierarchical or egalitarian. Relationship anarchy rejects predetermined hierarchies as a principle.

**Categories:** Polyamory usually maintains the distinction between partnership and friendship. Relationship anarchy deliberately dissolves this separation.

**Rules:** In polyamory, fixed rules and agreements are common. In relationship anarchy, arrangements are made individually and without a standard template.

## Which fits you?

There is no "better" or "worse" – only different answers to how you want to live closeness. A few guiding questions:

- Do you want clear structures and perhaps one main relationship? Then you may feel more comfortable in (possibly hierarchical) polyamory.
- Does it bother you that romantic relationships automatically outrank friendships? Then relationship anarchy may speak to you more.
- Do you want to define every connection from scratch, without labels? That is the core of relationship anarchy.

Many people move between these concepts over time – which is completely normal. Relationship models are not tattoos.

## Common questions

**Is relationship anarchy a form of polyamory?**
There is overlap, but it is increasingly treated as a distinct concept. Polyamory describes a relationship structure; relationship anarchy describes a fundamental stance towards all relationships.

**Can you be poly and a relationship anarchist at the same time?**
Yes. Many people combine both: multiple romantic relationships (poly) without fixed hierarchy between them (RA).

**Does relationship anarchy mean there are no rules?**
No. It rejects predetermined hierarchies, not responsibility or agreements. Those are simply negotiated individually.

**What is the difference between non-hierarchical poly and relationship anarchy?**
Non-hierarchical poly means treating all romantic relationships as equally valued. Relationship anarchy goes further: it removes the distinction between romantic and non-romantic relationships entirely.
